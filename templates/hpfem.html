What is hp-FEM?

The hp-FEM is a modern version of the Finite Element Method (FEM). It combines adaptively finite elements of variable size (h) and polynomial degree (p) to attain extremely fast, exponential convergence. This makes is vastly superior to standard low-order FEM methods which nowadays are abundant in both the academic and commercial software packages. The hp-FEM is more demanding than standard FEM from both the mathematical and programming point of view. Therefore, hp-FEM codes are rather scarce.

Example: The following graph corresponding to the Fichera corner problem is a representative example of the huge difference in convergence rates of adaptive low-order FEM (with linear and quadratic elements) and the hp-FEM. The horizontal and vertical axes show the number of degrees of freedom (unknowns in the discrete problem) and the error in logarithmic scale, respectively.

Our mission is to disseminate the hp-FEM broadly and to create an open-source environment that eventually will become an alternative to old commercial FEM codes.
