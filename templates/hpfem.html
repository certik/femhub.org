What is hp-FEM?

hp-FEM is a modern version of the Finite Element Method (FEM), which combines adaptively finite elements of variable size (h) and polynomial degree (p). With a good adaptivity algorithm, the hp-FEM attains extremely fast (exponential) convergence rates. Sample hp-FEM mesh is shown in the following image which corresponds to a flame propagation problem (enlarge using right click + view image).


The hp-FEM takes minutes where standard FEM takes hours or days. However, adaptive hp-FEM codes are rather scarce since the hp-FEM is more demanding than standard FEM from both the mathematical and programming point of view.

Example: The following graph corresponding to the Fichera corner problem is a representative example of the huge difference in convergence rates of adaptive low-order FEM (with linear and quadratic elements) and the hp-FEM. The horizontal and vertical axes show the number of degrees of freedom (unknowns in the discrete problem) and the error in logarithmic scale, respectively.
